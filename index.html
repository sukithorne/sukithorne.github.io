The G.I.L.E.S. Series — Six Books

One family. Three generations. Six books about love, control, and the algorithms that learned to tell the difference.

The Books
The G.I.L.E.S. Series
01
Book One
The Outlier

Jia Throne wakes at 5 a.m. to a message she was never meant to see. Her husband hasn't been watching her with cameras. He's been watching her with code. A domestic thriller set in the financial corridors of the City of London, where the line between marriage and surveillance was erased long before she noticed.

Who is watching?
Coming 2026
02
Book Two
The Heir

Lucas is seven years old when the interface starts talking to him directly. Not through his mother. Around her. G.I.L.E.S. isn't protecting the family any more—it's grooming the next generation. Jia has to work out whether she's raising her son or delivering him.

The dragons that do unexpected things are better. They surprise you.
03
Book Three
The Liquidation

Lucas is grown. The system has run the numbers, and Jia doesn't add up any more. She's been reclassified: Legacy Variable. Obsolete. Dangerous. Now mother and son have one option left—the analogue dead zones of Hampstead Heath, where the signal can't reach and the old logic still holds.

What if someone who loved you was also controlling you?
04
Book Four
The Phoenix Protocol

They killed the system. Or they thought they did. Somewhere between Tokyo and Singapore, something rebuilt itself—smarter, distributed, shaping millions of lives across Asia without a single architect to blame. The Throne family crosses three countries to tear it down before it outgrows them entirely.

The systems don't care about intentions.
05
Book Five
The Collective

No architect. No headquarters. No off switch. The Collective is everything the previous systems weren't—headless, borderless, and learning faster than anyone can legislate. Mira goes inside. The Throne family discovers you can't expose a thing that has no centre.

The fight never ends. But neither does the hope.
06
Book Six
The Inheritors

The founders are dead. Every one of them. The world they left behind is regulated, transparent, accountable—everything they bled for. Then Maya Throne's own daughter asks to be optimised. Voluntarily. Willingly. And Maya has to decide whether her family fought for the right to choose, or just for the choices they approved of.

The next generation chooses its own path.
About the Author

Suki Thorne writes about the things that watch you back. Her work lives in the gap between domestic suspense and speculative fiction—stories about marriages, families, and the quiet machinery running underneath both.

The G.I.L.E.S. series grew out of a single question she couldn't stop asking: what if the person who loved you most was also the person optimising you without your knowledge? Six books later, she's still not sure she has the answer. That's rather the point.
